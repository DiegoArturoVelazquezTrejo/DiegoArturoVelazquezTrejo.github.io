About

Hi there! My name is Diego Arturo Velázquez Trejo. I hold an M.Sc. in Biological Sciences (UNAM–INMEGEN) and I previously double majored in Applied Mathematics and Computer Sciences. My work sits at the intersection of quantitative modelling, machine learning, and genomic medicine, with a focus on extracting actionable biological insight from high-dimensional data and complex systems.

My research interests include drug repositioning and early-stage drug discovery, gene regulatory networks and regulatory inference in natural transcriptomes, and multi-omics integration across transcriptomics, proteomics, and metabolomics. I am also actively interested in antimicrobial resistance and data-driven strategies to accelerate therapeutic development and evaluation.

From a translational perspective, I work on metabolomics-based clinical applications—particularly gestational diabetes—where I collaborate with clinical and research teams to develop and validate quantitative approaches that enable earlier risk stratification and timely intervention. In parallel, I co-founded a startup focused on maturing a metabolomics-based prognostic test for early detection of gestational diabetes, bridging method development, clinical validation strategy, and technology transfer toward real-world deployment.

Data Analytics & AI Consulting

I provide end-to-end data analytics and applied AI consulting for teams that need rigorous, production-minded analysis and decision support. My work integrates statistical modelling, machine learning, and computational pipelines—from problem framing to deployment-ready deliverables— with particular strength in healthcare, life sciences, and high-dimensional data settings (omics, clinical cohorts, epidemiology, and time series).


Core Services

  • Data strategy & problem framing: translating business or research questions into measurable objectives, metrics, and study designs.

  • Data integration & preprocessing: ETL/ELT, schema alignment, missing data handling, de-duplication, QC, and reproducible pipelines.

  • Exploratory analysis & insights: statistical profiling, anomaly detection, cohort segmentation, and executive-ready interpretation.

  • Predictive modelling: supervised ML, risk scores, forecasting, classification/regression, and model selection under constraints.

  • Bayesian analytics: MCMC-based inference, uncertainty quantification, hierarchical models, and decision-making under uncertainty.

  • Optimization & simulation: stochastic simulation, scenario analysis, sensitivity analysis, and numerical optimization for planning.

  • Dashboards & reporting: clear visual narratives, KPI tracking, and communication for technical and non-technical stakeholders.

Specialized Domains


  • Life sciences & health: omics analytics (transcriptomics/proteomics/metabolomics), differential analysis, clustering, and network inference.

  • Clinical & epidemiological data: retrospective cohorts, risk stratification, survival/time-to-event concepts, and reproducible analyses.

  • Time series & longitudinal data: panel/time series modelling, window detection, drift detection, and intervention analyses.

  • Network science: gene regulatory networks, graph-based modelling, and interpretable network metrics for prioritization.

  • Drug-focused analytics: similarity-based screening, repositioning pipelines, and integration of chemical/biological evidence.

Engagement Model

  1. Discovery (1–2 sessions): objectives, constraints, data audit, and definition of success metrics.

  2. Blueprint: analysis plan, timeline, deliverables, and risk/assumption register.

  3. Build: data pipeline + modelling + validation with iterative checkpoints.

  4. Delivery: report + visuals + reproducible code/notebooks + handover session.

  5. Support: optional maintenance, monitoring, and iteration as new data arrives.

Deliverables are designed to be reproducible, auditable, and easy to transfer to internal teams.

Typical Deliverables

  • Cleaned and documented dataset + data dictionary

  • Reproducible notebooks/scripts (Python/R) and version-controlled repository

  • Model report: performance, assumptions, limitations, and interpretability

  • Decision-ready dashboard/figures + KPI definitions

  • Technical brief for stakeholders + recommendations and next steps

Who I Work With

  • Startups: product analytics, metrics, experimentation, and ML-enabled features.

  • Clinical/biomedical teams: cohort analytics, biomarker modelling, omics workflows, and translational research support.

  • Organizations with operational data: forecasting, segmentation, and optimization for planning and resource allocation.
